103.2
You find an ancient stairway cut into the rock and follow
it down, where you discover a fountain, empty and lifeless
except for a few piles of dust and a strange piece of armor.
Though dirt and water stains blot the surface, it shines in the
secondhand sunlight.

“Looks like it’s made of glass,” says Ed, picking it up. “But it
weighs almost nothing.”

Gain adventure card 64 (Ghost Bracer).

You wander along a ledge to another ancient structure. The
shattered buildings must have once been magnificent, but
intricate columns and broken statues are all that’s left of a
great palace.

A bit of smoke drifts at the edge of your vision. Is there a fire
nearby?

“Did you see that?” asks Miguel.

“This place is thick with spirits,” says Ezarius, his shoulders
tensed.

More smoke drifts through the hall. It gathers together and
forms into headless people in tattered clothes, ethereal and
flowing as if underwater. Their heads suddenly appear:
cracked, floating insect heads.

“Disgusting,” says Jessie, backing away as the ghosts surround
you.

COMBAT 18, 58, 59, 60 (Level 7)
You may run from this combat at the end of any enemy phase. If
you do, return to the map.
(If you defeat the enemies, turn to 103.3)

103.3

103.3
An obsidian statue of a harkian tilts above a severe drop in
the canyon. Empty stone bookshelves surround the statue.

“Where are the books? Where are the scrolls?” lmirian says.
“They’ve all been stolen! They must have been stolen!”

“They wouldn’t last long out in the open like this,” says Ed,
brushing dust from one of the shelves.

“What’s this?” asks Jessie, pointing to a small sparrow figurine
on the floor.

lmirian glances at it, crestfallen. “A mere knickknack. A piece
of junk left from when thieves emptied this place.”

The sparrow’s eyes start to glow. A voice projects from the
tiny open beak, but it sounds as if an ancient scholar were
speaking.

“Inquire away, and I will erase your delusions,” says the sparrow.

lmirian stares with an open mouth. “This can’t be right,” he
whinnies.

“I am the knowledge keeper of the harkians,” says the sparrow.
“I was made to keep the knowledge safe over many generations,
before this whispering city was destroyed by Orf ash
and cursed by Ohmlude for rejecting the gods.”

lmirian picks up the sparrow, his eyes filled with a guarded
hope. “Tell me, who made the harkians? Was it Orfash?”

“Orfash and Ohmlude together created the harkians,” says
the sparrow.

lmirian stamps his feet. “I knew it! I knew it wasn’t only Orfash!
Ohmlude is the goddess of speech, singing, and poetry.
She would never give a speech limit! I must take this back to
my people. There is so much to learn.”

When you climb the narrow pathways and broken stairs back
to the plateau, the plane is waiting for you. lmirian grips your
hand.

“Here, as promised,” he says. “You have started a new era for
us, I hope you realize.” He hands you a small pebble covered
in strange markings.

“No need to give us all the credit,” says Jessie. “I wouldn’t
want Ekeren hunting us down to properly thank us with a
spear in the back.”

lmirian shakes his head. “I’ll keep you out of the rest of this, I
swear. He won’t be bothering you.”

Ed mutters. “I know people back home who would pay a high
price for such a toy, er, artifact. Are we sure we want to let
him keep it?”

Complete quest 36. Gain quest 23. Lose adventure card 37
(lmirian). Gain 2 tundra berries, 1 bloodflower, 1 fire quartz, and 1
jadeworm egg. Return to the map.
