You jump into the roaring water, struggling against the rush.

body. Ezarius climbs onto a ledge and helps you up as you
gasp for breath.

The river disappears into a misty chasm, howling as it falls into
the darkness. Hanging from the ceiling, like a bead of water,

the cave’s dark throat.

“ONLY TRUE WORSHIPERS OF SHORME ENTER HERE.”

was made by Shorme, goddess of stone and the fire of the
deep earth. We must leave now.”

11.1

11.1
A dead body floats through your wake. It twitches suddenly,
crawls onto a rock, then stands, filling the air with its putrid
odor. You recoil at the stringy flesh and bulging eyes.

“You’re from Henrik’s world,” the corpse gurgles. “Searching
for totems too? Meecra promised him they would give her
the power to send him home. But she won’t do that. Meecra’s
hiding something,” she taunts.

The soggy zombie trudges into the water, infecting the
surface with chaotic ripples. “My strength is waning. If you
wish to know more, you must summon my soul from the spirit
world. Ascend the mountains and find the temple to the west
of the God Eye. Give the monk an ogroot and some meat, and
he’ll tell you what to do. Goodbye, for now.” The corpse flops
into the lake and disappears in a cloud of silt and algae.

Complete quest 212. Gain quest 7. Gain 1 sand honey and 1 meat.
Return to the map.

11.3

11.3
Sand crunches beneath your feet. A cold mist, pouring down
from the mountain, blankets the shore ahead, where something
red catches your eye. It’s a tackle box surrounded by
bits of gear-a fishing pole, a tin can filled with worms, a plate,
and some rusty utensils.

As you pick through the box, a wild cry rings out. Two human
forms burst from the fog, scrambling desperately along the
beach.

Heavy steps shake the ground. From behind the veil of fog, a
titanic cyclops lurches forward, swinging arms like wrecking
balls. Its one terrible eye shines with primeval rage above
gnashing teeth and a tongue that wriggles like a live snake.
Two blood-stained horns hang from the monster’s head. The
twins cannot escape his tree trunk fingers as he scoops them
up and pounds off into the fog bank.

Ed and Miguel shudder behind a rock, peering after the
cyclops. Jessie is frozen, her feet planted in the sand of the
lakeshore, gripping her baseball bat pitifully.

Miguel stares aghast at the fading forms. “Those are the
twins! They’re going to be cyclops food!”

“We have to help them,” says Ed. “But this feels like a losing
hand.”

“There are ways to take down a cyclops,” says Ezarius. “But
not with meager weapons. We need to be prepared. It will
not be an easy fight or an easy journey. The cyclops roam the
tundra, north of the canyon. But we need strong weapons if
we’re going to defeat him.”

Complete quest 5. Gain quest 8. Gain adventure card 23 (Fishing
Pole). Return to the map.
