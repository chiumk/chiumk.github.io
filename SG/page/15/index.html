The mining guild office is a cramped building, its shelves lined
with boxes of jagged minerals, the floor covered in slate chips
and mining dust. The smell of old oil permeates every chair
and hanging lantern. A man in a deep red coat sits at a desk
covered in tools and papers and bits of junk.

“Come to ask for jobs? We’re having issues with the zarg rats.
They’re stealing our tools and causing all sorts of problems.
We just found their hidden village and we’re planning an
operation to remove them from the canyon. It’s a one-time job
and the pay is good.”

A. Tell him you’ll join the mercenaries.
(Turn to 32.1)

negotiate.

Travelers in torn cloaks gather at the edge of the boiling lake,
their wrists and ankles draped with rough stones.

“This pilgrimage started in the city of Alzarria,” says one of
them. “We’ve come to burn our hands in the boiling lakes and
geysers. These are natural temples to the goddess Shorme.
She controls the fires beneath the earth.”

“She’s lost so much of her power. She can’t protect you. Why
do you worship such a tyrant?” says Ezarius. “She destroyed
much of my home city.”

“How dare you speak such blasphemy! She destroyed Lukra
City because of your sins and disobedience. Because you did
not worship her.”

“She’s a petty tyrant and deserves to be destroyed,” says
Ezarius. “If she were here right now, I would kill her myself.”

“You sound like a Red Heretic! You’re an enemy of the gods!”
The pilgrims come at you, swinging the rough stones with
surprising velocity.
