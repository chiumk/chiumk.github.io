50~
Clouds blanket the night sky, growling with faraway thunder.

OBSTACLE: Darkness
Lose 1 torch OR take 5 damage and gain frightened.

As you search for shelter from the coming storm, Ed halts,
staring into the darkness ahead. “I thought I saw my wife out
there.” He wanders into the dark mist.

You dash after him, tripping through the tall grass, over roots
and gnarled branches. Suddenly, the mist clears, and you stop
to face three large demons, shiny and black, with mouths of
square, pearly teeth. One of the demons holds Ed by the leg.

“Do you think he’s tasty? I haven’t had a good meal in ages!”

“Yes, I’m so tired of eating squirrels,” says another.

As they lift Ed toward their disgusting mouths, you call out.
“Stop!”

A. Make a deal with the demons to let Ed go.

(Turn to 50.1)

B. Fight the demons!

51.1

51.1 ~
King Trusiam sits on a tall throne at the end of a long hallway,
the architecture so exaggeratedly large that it’s more ridiculous
than grand. As you draw closer, you can see that his
crown is too large for him and hangs low over one side of his
forehead. He sits crookedly on his throne, body bent with age,
the heavy layers of fine fabrics he wears weighing him down
further. But his eyes are clear and sharp, and his expression is
haughty and full of pride.

You bow before the king, but he does not respond. Instead,
another man, not quite as bent with age, stands and greets
you. He introduces himself as Count Heilynn, descendant of
a long line of nobility stretching back to the old Cadwahlyn
kingdom itself.

“Have you come to fulfill the king’s quests?” The count looks
just as haughty and proud as the king.

A. Present the Sodden Sword.
Requires keyword SWORD.
(Turn to 51.12)

Requires keyword CUP.
(Turn to 51.13)

C. Present the trophy of the Harrowed Hart.
Requires keyword HART.
(Turn to 51.2)

D. Say a polite goodbye.
Return to the map.
