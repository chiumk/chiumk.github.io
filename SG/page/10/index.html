18.9
Eedarah lies in the ice, coughing through reddened lips. Blood
runs from her mouth down her chin, mixing into a gash made
by one of Thrack’s thick talons.

“This is the end for me. I die knowing there’s a little less evil in
the world.”

“Nol You will not die!” In a flash of feathers, Hessrah turns
into a giant falcon and grips Eedarah in her talons, flapping
through the cave toward the surface. “I will take her to my
father Kemter! He will heal her!” she calls out.

“I couldn’t stand it if she dies!” says Jessie, her forehead
creased in worry.

“Kemter is the god of growth and plants. Perhaps he will heal
her, though I hold little hope. The gods are treacherous,” says
Ezarius.

Gain adventure cards 89 (Thrack’s Heart) and 90 (Thrack’s Brain).
Complete quests 53 and 78. Lose adventure cards 51 (Eedarah)
and 88 (Hessrah). Return to the map.

19~
As you approach the mountain, you find a cave like the mouth
of a sleeping giant tucked beneath the stone. It yawns warm
pockets of air which keep the ice and snow at bay and thaw
the surrounding earth. A congregation of mountain grasses
and little clumps of moss gathers happily in the black mud.

“It’s not Honolulu,” says Jessie, “But I love it!”

“Hold on,” warns Ed. “A warm cave up here must have something
living inside.”

OBSTACLE: Darkness
Lose 1 torch OR take 9 damage and gain frightened.

The cave winds into a chamber where large, light green worms
clothed in overlapping scales crawl across the dewy walls.
Jessie starts when she sees them, but Ezarius walks into the
room, unafraid.

“These are jadeworms. They won’t hurt you,” he says, moving
to a nest made of transparent crystals. Inside is a large, green
egg, but you can’t retrieve it without breaking the nest.

A. Cut through the nest to retrieve the egg.
OCRAFT 17
Fail: -15 health. Gain weakened. (Giant pieces of crystal nest fall on
you.)
(Turn to 19.1)

B. Leave.
Return to the map.

20.1

20.1
You meet the mayor of Milius town, a tall woman with two
heavy braids.

“You people look like you come from one of the crashed flying
machines. Yes? I don’t understand where you come from,
but people like you keep showing up here. There was a man
named Bernard staying at the inn. He gathered a group of
hunters together-said they were going to kill the Helthark. I
hope I don’t need to tell you what a terrible idea that is.”

“No one here is interested in a quick death,” you say. “But can
you tell us more about this group of hunters?”

“What I’m most worried about is that Bernard and his hunters
will anger the Helthark. The giant creature might exact
revenge on our little town. Ten years ago, a group of fanatics
were looking for the Helthark’s nest. All they wanted was
to worship the beast, but they ventured too close, and the
titan crushed them in a rockslide. Then it flew over here and
dropped a boulder near one of the houses. We were lucky-it
was meant as a warning, even though we had nothing to do
with the fanatics.”

“Can’t you pacify the beast?” asks Ed. “Show it you mean it
no harm?”

“Some have tried, but none survive. The creature is ancient,
and its purpose is singular-to guard the God Eye. Nothing to
do but leave it alone. Perhaps you can find Bernard before it’s
too late.”

Gain quest 65. Return to the map.

in the legends of King Anfortas. The historian, a woman
named Etan, lets you in, but tells you she can’t help you right

all night to find it, because, well. .. “ Etan gestures towards
the whole room, which is an absolute mess of books, loose
papers, and various odds and ends.

“Alright, alright, there’s no need to raise your voice. You need
to know about the white stag, do you? As a young man King
Anfortas pursued it for years, but never caught it. Some view

“This is a recreation of an old Cadwahlyn hunting horn. This
is what King Anfortas would have used to call the hunt. Just
blow on this end here if you want it to make a noise. Now
please be on your way, I really must find that thesis.”

22~
Near the edge of the village a man with the head of a stallion
is chopping wood. The harkian neighs softly when he sees
you, then beckons you closer. “A funny man visited the village
a week ago. He said he was from another world. He was
dressed a bit like you. Do you know him? He said his name
was George Gift.”

Jessie’s eyes light up. “The actor? I just watched one of his
pictures at the Coliseum Theatre! He’s famous!”

“He can’t be that famous if I haven’t heard of him,” scoffs
Miguel.

“You wouldn’t, ‘cause he doesn’t do stuffy old silent films,” she
scoffs.

“Do you know where he went?” Ed asks the harkian, ignoring
the squabble.

“He bought supplies for an expedition. He was headed to
the rocky spires just northeast of town, and he convinced a
few harkians to go with him, too. He mentioned something
about a rare treasure, and he had a madness about him like he
couldn’t hold his thoughts together without great effort.”

Gain quest 34. Cross off location 22. Return to the map.
