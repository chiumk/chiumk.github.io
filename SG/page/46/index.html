You’re surprised by Miguel’s skill in finding the hives and
extracting the honey without being stung. He laughs when he
sees your confused expression.

“When I was very young, my grandparents kept bees,” he explains.
“They used the honey for cocktails in their bar before it
went under. I was too young for the cocktails, but that honey
was the sweetest I’ve ever tasted.”

From the way he speaks you can tell Miguel felt homesick long
before he came to this world.

Gain 2 sand honey. Return to the map.

92~
A dignified granite dragon carved high up in the side of a wall
shoots clear water from its mouth in a graceful arc. The water
hangs in the air, sparkling in the sun for just a moment, before
it tumbles down to fill the azure fountain below.

A nearby sign reads “Drinking Water,” but you don’t see anyone
drinking from the fountain. In fact, the locals seem to give
the fountain a wide berth, not even looking at it for too long
before hurrying away to their various destinations.

An old woman, hunched over and wrapped in rags, hobbles
up to you, searching your face with pleading eyes. “I’m so
thirsty,” she says. “Can’t you help me to get a drink?”

A. Give the woman some of your own water.
(Turn to 92.1)

B. Offer the woman some water from the fountain.
(Turn to 92.2)

C. Refuse to help the woman.
(Turn to 92.3)

You’re thrown unceremoniously into a stone-walled cell with
a wooden door. The guards march away, leaving behind only
a weasel-faced jailer who squints at you through the barred
window in the door of your damp cell.

“Bad luck,” says the jailer. “King Trusiam has been complaining
that the dungeons are too empty, so the guards started
targeting people near that fountain.”

None of you are happy about this, but Ed seems especially
furious.

“This is outrageous! We’re entitled to a lawyer. Someone to
speak in our defense at the trial.”

The jailer laughs. “There won’t be a trial, friend. You’re stuck
here indefinitely. Unless you happen to have friends in high
places, or we could come to some sort of arrangement.” The
jailer waggles his eyebrows at you and holds his hand out
expectantly.
