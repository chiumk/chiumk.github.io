108[!1}
If you have adventure card 104 (Venme), turn to 108.4.

You land in a foggy canyon near a rolling river. A small cottage
sits in the grass, the plaster cracked and crumbling. Roots and
herbs hang from a drooping line, and a woman sits nearby,
weaving a basket.

“That’s a very nice airplane you’ve got there,” she says.

Ezarius raises an eyebrow. “And how do you know about
airplanes?”

“My grandfather was from Earth. And eight years ago I
traveled on a lost ship from there. The crew told me all about
airplanes.”

“A ship from Earth? Did they ever get back home?’’ asks
Jessie.

“If they did, it means they found the totems they were looking
for. Is that what you’re here for too?” says the woman, setting
down the basket. She sighs when she sees the expectant
looks on your faces.

“I think I can help you. But I want you to take me back to
Earth. I’ve always wanted to see what it was like.”

“Even if can’t come back?” says Miguel, surprised. “You
wouldn’t miss anyone?”

“There’s nothing left for me here. It’s time for something new,”
says the woman. She says her name is Venme and tells you
how a totem that belonged to her grandfather ended up stolen
by a group of villagers nearby.

A. Navigate the fog at the base of the canyon to reach the
village.

The village lies in steppes at the top of the canyon where slate
houses wear mossy roofs like fat caps. In the town square, a
man in a rusty breastplate confronts you while a ragtag group
of villagers and beasts looks on.

“Venme, I said you can’t have the stone. We need it to help
our gardens grow!” he bellows.

“You don’t need it, Ergom. Its power is long gone. Your
gardens suffer from neglect and nothing more,” says Venme.

The man looks at your crew. “I see you brought mercenaries.

“That’s up to you.”

“If we don’t teach you a lesson now, you’ll keep coming back!”

108.3
The villagers retreat, and you corner the man in the rusty
breastplate at the edge of a cliff. A waterfall roars behind him,
disappearing into the mist below. He grips the totem in his
bloodied hand and reaches out over the falls.

“If we can’t have it, neither can you!” he screams. Without a
pause, he throws the totem into the waterfall, then looks back
at you with venomous satisfaction.

Venme sighs. “Why did you have to go and be so dramatic? It
wasn’t worth it, anyway. It has no power.”

“You lie!” says the man.

“Listen, I’m leaving this canyon,” says Venme. “My house is
filled with seeds and my garden has rich soil. Use what you
can to help your village.”

The man shakes his head, a dumbfounded look on his face,
and you return to the plane.

Gain adventure card 104 (Venme). Draw 1 ability card and gain 1

“But can we trust Meecra?” Miguel’s words to Ed reach your
ear, and you stop to listen. “What’s to stop her from taking
all the totems we find and leaving us to rot in the mountains
while the God Eye closes?’’

“Henrik told us what would happen if we tried to fly through
the portal without her help,” says Ed. “We don’t have much of
a choice.”

Ed watches Ezarius, who is sitting quietly out of earshot. “I’m
more worried about our Lukran friend over there. He’s been
holding his cards close to the chest. Let’s hope he plays a
hand in our favor when all of this is over.”

Later that night, as you sit down to rest near the coals of your
campfire, Meecra’s pearl eyes and starry-night hair glint from
the shadows.

“You defeated Orfash once, but he will attack us again before
this is all over. With the power of the totems, we can defeat
him and remove your curse. Do you still have hallucinations?”

“Yes, but they’re not as common,” you tell her.

“This is a good sign. It means Orfash’s strength is waning as
well. When you have restored at least five totems, meet me at
the graveyard of flying machines north of Shorme’s Geysers.”

Meecra disappears as the moon hides behind a cloud.

Complete quest 131. Gain quest 132. Gain 1 tundra berry. Return to
the map.

110 [!1l
Meecra visits you at dusk, her eyes like moonlight shining in
the dim.

“Orfash put a curse on Claire. Why didn’t you help us? Can
you heal her?” says Miguel.

Meecra examines the curse-wound that still burns on your
neck. “I cannot cure this wound in my current state. And I’m
sorry I couldn’t help you face Orfash. I was too weak and he
would have killed me.”

“Why are you so weak? What happened to the gods? Weren’t
they strong?” asks Miguel.

“Yes, they were, in ancient times, and they were strong when
the gods were awakened by the Earth woman named Sofi
Odessa eight years ago. But a group called the Red Heretics
found a way to weaken the gods by destroying their totems.
Most of them are Alzarrian, but members of many nations are
secret Red Heretics. They search the world over for more totems,
for they wish to destroy the gods forever,” says Meecra.

“But if the Red Heretics kill you, we won’t be able to get
home,” says Jessie.

Ezarius looks uncomfortable. “Yes, but we need not worry
about them. They’re far away from here.”

“Not so far. They often hide who they are, but you’ll meet
many of them in this land,” says Meecra, pausing to look at
each of you. “I fear you must defeat Orfash if you wish to remove
this curse. My visions tell me he has gone to the boiling
lake in the northeast to meet with Shorme, the goddess of
earth and fire.”

Before leaving, Meecra gives you another dormant totem, a
mysterious crystal, a map, and a sword made of coral.

Complete quest 129. Gain quests 126, 130, and 208. Gain adventure
cards 130 and 133. Return to the map.

Rust-red cliffs jut out of the desert, like stone puzzle pieces in
a giant sandbox. Eugene points out a flat patch on one of the
cliffs to land on, though you barely miss a few of the twisted,
dead trees when the wheels hit the rough runway.

“The mud seer said to look beneath the blind eye,” says Miguel.
“You don’t suppose she was speaking literally?”

“Not sure,” says Jessie, climbing down from the plane. “Maybe
whoever lives over there would know.”

A small pyramid sits on an adjacent cliff, built of the same
rusty stone. The construction is sloppy; a drunk snake could
slither a straighter line than the edges and corners of the
structure. There’s a large gap between your side and the rock
spires that hold the pyramid on their red shoulders.

111.1

111.1
Sweat drips from your face and slaps the stones at the entrance
of the pyramid, a dark slit letting you spy the bone-littered
floor within.

“Come in,” says a reedy voice.

Jessie hesitates, holding her baseball bat in a white-knuckled
grip, and edges forward. An old hag sits inside, her arms and
hands so long they reach all the way past her chair to the
floor, resting on the dirty tile. Ratty hair runs to her navel and
in her snakeskin face are two dark sockets where eyes should
be, blood dripping, constantly dripping from the withered lids.
Miguel gasps.

“What are you?” asks Ezarius.

The hag laughs. “Oh, don’t be put off by my appearance,
young ones. To many, I’m considered beautiful. At least I was,
until my eye was stolen.”

You glance at the bones littering the floor-rat and bird mostly.
“Don’t you ever clean the place?” you ask.

“Yes, but it’s much more difficult when I’m missing my eye.
And I’m not the one who leaves the bones. It’s my pets, you
see. They need help, and I take care of them, though they
don’t like strangers, so they’re hiding now.” The hag sweeps
the floor with her large hands, brushing bones away from her
stone chair. “But I know why you’ve come. The mud seer sent
you, didn’t she? Sent you to get a totem. But I won’t part with
it unless you help me get that eye back from the historians.”

Ezarius folds his arms. “You mean there are others around
here? Who would live in this dead place?”

“Yes, they call themselves historians. They say they have
come to study the ancient gods. But they hunt my pets and
they stole my eye, so I don’t like them.”

A. Help the hag reclaim her eye.
(Turn to 111.2)

B. Threaten the hag and tell her to give you the totem she has.
(Turn to 111.10)

The hag tells you where the historians are camped, and you
follow the maze of canyons until you reach an expanse of
cracked earth. By now, the sun is turning the cloudless sky
orange, and you soon see the camp ahead. White tents and
wooden houses rest at the foot of a boulder.

A sudden shout pierces the silent dusk. In moments, men and
women in tall caps pour out of the camp and surround you,
pointing loaded crossbows at your throats.

“The University of Lukra sent you, didn’t they? Go back and
tell them they cannot stop our quest for knowledge,” says one
of them.

Jessie throws a confused look at Ezarius, who shakes his
head. “You have it all wrong. I’m simply a trader. We’ve heard
that you have a hag’s eye, and we’re willing to negotiate for it.”

The historians mutter to themselves. One of them spits on
the ground. Another takes a book from his pocket and tears a
page from it, at horrified gasps from his colleagues.

“The desert witch! As soon as we got here she sent her creatures
after us. They’ve killed many of our best scholars. And
we’ve come peacefully, simply to study this place and find
secrets about the ancient gods. And she lets her pets eat our
dead. Did she mention that?”

Another scholar steps forward with a small purse. “Listen
strangers, if you leave us and return to where you came from, I
can give you something for your trouble.”
