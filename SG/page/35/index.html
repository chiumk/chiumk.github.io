65.4 ~
Tall buildings loom on either side of this small side street, casting
deep shadows over the strangely quiet stalls. Buyers and
sellers conduct their business in whispers, glaring at you every
time you dare to look at them.

Ezarius tells you to keep your eyes down. “In the Shaded Market,
a stray glance can make dangerous enemies. The rule here
is that you will go unseen as long as you do not see.”

But Miguel has already wandered off and is staring intently at
the offerings at a particular stall.

“Ed!” he shouts, drawing more than just Ed’s attention. “You’ll
want to see this!” Miguel holds up a small landscape painting
depicting an impressionist version of a wheat field-a piece
from Ed’s collection that he had been planning to sell in Honolulu.

The owner of the stall is a gray-haired woman with a missing
ear and a deep scar across one cheek. She introduces herself
as Thadri.

“It’s a pretty picture, eh? If you want it, I’ll trade you for a fire
quartz or something better. Otherwise, don’t waste my time.”

A. Intimidate Thadri into revealing where she got the painting.

You give Thadri an emotional plea about the stolen paintings
and the sentimental value of the collection. Thadri seems
unimpressed.

“Look, I don’t ask where my suppliers get the stuff, I just sell it
on at reasonable prices. If you’re going to kick up a fuss about
theft and legal ownership, I may have to call over a few of my
friends to show you out of the market.”

Thadri makes a gesture, and a few nearby toughs take a few
steps closer.

“Please, Thadri,” interjects Ed. “I know what it’s like to run a
business dealing in products that occasionally have dubious
origins. We’re not here to make trouble for you. But you must
also know what it’s like to have a few items you truly could
not bear to part with, under any circumstances-personal
treasures that transcend business concerns. I’m only asking for
your help to retrieve one of my personal treasures.”
