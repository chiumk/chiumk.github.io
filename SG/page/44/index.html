87~
“Watch out for the ofilogs,” says Ezarius, pointing across the
frozen landscape. “They can be vicious when provoked.”

The ofilogs, massive scaled walruses with four eyes each, are
basking in the sun just outside of a large hole in the ice-and
sticking halfway out of the water is an overturned metal craft
shaped like a nautilus. Supply crates are scattered like dice on
the surrounding ice.

The ofilogs blink their eyes lazily. One of them turns over onto
its back. Either they haven’t noticed you, or they don’t mind
spectators.

A. Sneak past the ofilogs to search the crates.

disturbed.)

disturbed.)

You survey the ofilog herd from a safe distance, looking for
any animal that looks noticeably different.

Ed stomps his feet and hugs himself against the cold. “This is
impossible! The creatures all look the same.”

“Here!” Jessie points to a deep groove in the snow. “These
could be ofilog tracks! Maybe we should follow them.”

The tracks lead you to a cave carved into a pillar of ice. A
deep roar echoes from within its depths as you approach.

Miguel groans. “Is that sound what I think it is?”

“Yes.” Ezarius draws his weapon. “Prepare yourselves!”

When the creature finally emerges from the cave, there’s no
doubt in your mind that this is the one you’re looking for.

As the sun sets, the empty mansion comes to life. Lanterns
glow in the windows. Townsfolk and travelers approach the
door, where they either hand over a paper invitation or pay a

large hat and ask her what’s going on.

but they’re letting anyone in with just a small donation to the
Milius Miners and Merchants Concern. I simply couldn’t miss
the opportunity!”
