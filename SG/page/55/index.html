114 ~
As you fly in, you can see the smoke and flames from abovethe
village of Pran is on fire.

Ezarius is unable to take his eyes away from the burning town.
“Hurry, please! There are so many people down there!”

But as you rush to the village’s aid, you’re blocked just outside
the gates by a group of robed figures, their indistinct bodies
bristling with weapons of all sorts. A towering man steps forward.
Underneath his hood, he has the face of a bull. When
he speaks, his voice is a low rumble.

“Turn back! This village suffers a just punishment for harboring
heretics. We will allow no interference.”

Before you have time to respond, Ezarius shouts a curse, pulls
out his weapon, and charges forward.

114.1
The cultists defeated, Ezarius picks up the axe of the fallen
bull-man and tosses it to you.

“Let’s go,” he growls, before running forward into the smoldering
town.

It takes the rest of the night, but working together with the
villagers you manage to put out all the fires and chase away
the remaining cultists. But Ezarius still looks unsettled. He
stares into every face and every corner, looking for something
or someone he cannot find.

Jessie watches his continued panic with sympathy. “Is there
anything more we can do to help?”

Ezarius looks like he’s about to answer, but he changes his
mind, closing himself off again. He turns away. “It’s been a
long night and you’ve all done so much already. You should all
get some rest. I can keep searching on my own.”

“Nonsense,” says Ed, although he’s visibly exhausted. “If
there’s work that still needs to be done, we’ll do it.”

115 ~
As you try to hand out pamphlets to passersby, talking loudly
about the evils of King Trusiam, the people of Meriok largely
ignore you. Some of them glance at you and roll their eyes
before hurrying on. Some of them give you a more sympathetic
look, but they still won’t stop to talk to you.

Finally, a young man comes up and claps you on the back.
“Hey, folks. We appreciate what you’re doing here, but most
of the citizens of Meriok have already heard of us and what
we’re doing. Plus, it can be pretty dangerous to openly show
interest in the rebellion this close to the city. Here.” The young
man takes the stack of fliers from you.

“I’ll put these somewhere where people are more likely to
accept them. Secretly, of course. Maybe it will still make a bit
of a difference. I know a few fence-sitters who might be interested
in what our philosophers have to say. Thanks again for
helping out! We appreciate our volunteers, even when they’re
a bit misguided.”

As soon as the young man is out of sight, Ed sighs. “That kid
sure was condescending. At least we got that humiliating task
over with.”

You stand in an alleyway, offering pamphlets to passersby using
sideways language and careful euphemisms. But much to
your surprise, the people of Tharkholm are highly receptive to
your message, and bold in their declarations of support. One
young woman takes a whole stack of pamphlets from you.

“My grandparents told me stories of how much worse the
working conditions in the mines got after Trusiam took power,
and I saw for myself how he just kept pushing the miners
farther and farther, paying them less and less to do more and
more work. My own father died in one of those mines, before
most of them closed. It’s because of leaders like Trusiam that
those mines are so full of ghosts now.” The young woman’s
eyes shine with a revolutionary fervor.

“I know exactly who will want to read these pamphlets. I may
not be an official rebel, but I appreciate what you’re doing.
Truly.”

The people of Milius rush back and forth, busy with their
grand plans, their ambitious projects, and their get-rich-quick
schemes. Almost no one spares you a second glance. A few
curious intellectual-types, mostly anthropologists from Lukra
University, stop to listen to you and accept the pamphlets
you’re handing out.

this?” This last question was directed towards Ezarius.

“I’m not involved. But I support anyone’s effort to overthrow
their oppressors.” Despite his casual affect, there’s a dangerous
spark in Ezarius’ eyes.

“Truly fascinating,” says the Lukran scholar.

You journey to the graveyard of airplanes. The God Eye-the
portal to your home-swirls in the distant sky.

As the sun wanes and the night frogs begin their chants,
Meecra watches you with white eyes from shadows as dark as
an ink well.

“The time has come-the portal will close soon. Tell me you
have the totems,” she says, striding forward, her feet barely
leaving prints on the ground.

“Yes,” you say, digging into your bag. You rummage through
bits of rope, matches, and notes, but you can’t find the totems.
Jessie pulls out her pockets and badgers Ed and Miguel,
but no one has them. A smile gathers at the corner of Meeera’s
mouth.

“I know where they are,” she says, looking at Ezarius.

He stares back silently, hesitation in his eyes and on the edge
of his tongue.

“Well, then give her the totems,” says Jessie. “I want to go
home!”

“I can’t do that,” says Ezarius, now with growing determination.

“What are you talking about? Quit wasting time and give her
the totems!” says Miguel.

“He won’t do that,” says Meecra. “You see, he is a Red Heretic.
He will only use the totems to weaken the gods. Shorme
destroyed his home, and he wants revenge.”

Ezarius looks almost relieved at Meecra’s revelation. His
tensed shoulders relax a little.

“How long have you known?” he asks.

“Since we met,” says Meecra. “But it didn’t trouble me. I started
the Red Heretics in the first place.”

Ezarius returns an expression of guarded disbelief. “Why
would you create something that would weaken you and all
the gods?”

Meecra sighs. “I could no longer watch the world suffer. There
was too much pain and death and I was willing to put myself
in danger to stop it.

“Ages ago, before a great sleep fell upon us, Shorme and
Valard destroyed the vast Pann empire. Now only a few pitiful
nomads remain. When the gods were awakened eight years
ago, they crushed nations in their rage. Shorme killed thousands
in the city of Lukra. The gods have lived above mortals
for too long. When I discovered the way to weaken them, I
had to share it.”

“Why didn’t you tell us earlier?” asks Jessie.

“Orfash is already hunting me because I destroyed his temple.
If the gods knew my secret, they would destroy me. I only
share this with you now because I must.”

Ezarius wears a troubled expression now. “What proof do you
have? How can I trust you?”

“The Red Heretics have long kept the secret of how to destroy
a totem. They wait to use it when the time is right. But I gave
them the secret to begin with. Do you know what it is?”

“Of course,” says Ezarius. “But do you?”

“Only the venom of the Hectakron can destroy a totem. And
no mortal has ever procured it. Where do you think those
vials in your guarded care came from. I gave them to the
Heretics.”

Ezarius has no reply. Meecra’s evidence is enough to silence
his doubts. But Miguel has other worries.

“You’ve lied to us this whole time,” he says. “You were going
to take the totems for yourself. How could you so easily betray
us? “

“It did not worry me at first. But it has become a heavier burden.
If what Meecra says is true, maybe we can all get what
we want.”

“To destroy the gods?” asks Miguel.

Ezarius shakes his head. “To destroy them completely is to
become like them-cruel, destructive, heartless. I don’t want
to murder the gods, but I do expect them to live as equals.”

“And where does that leave us?” you ask.

In a sudden explosion of flame and smoke, Orfash roars,
his horns eternally burning, his eyes black with bloodlust.
“Shorme! Ohmlude! Where are you?”

Two more gods appear, one of them a short, curly-haired
woman with a fishing pole, the other a pale woman with obsidian
hair and ember eyes.

“What’s this about?” The short woman swashes the fishing
pole in his face. “I have no interest in your little feud with
Meecra.”

Orfash snorts. “Listen, Ohmlude, I’ve had my suspicions for
weeks, but now I’m certain. I can prove Meecra has been
scheming with mortals to betray the gods.” Shorme looks
intrigued, watching Meecra with deadly eagerness.

Ohmlude sighs. “Not this again. Why would she do something
like that? She’s as weak as the rest of us.”

Orfash stomps the ground with a heavy hoof, sending chunks
of rock and dirt in every direction. “Because she’s sided with
the mortals! She wants to be one of them. This Lukran she’s
working with is a Red Heretic!”

Ohmlude throws down the fishing pole and waddles up to
Ezarius, grabbing the edge of his coat and sniffing it. “Yes!
Orfash is right. This man has the smell of Hectakron venom on
him. It’s in one of his pockets!”

Shorme turns to Meecra, rage in her eyes. “Traitor! How dare
you work against us!”

Meecra nods solemnly. “Ezarius, now is the time. Give me the
totems. It’s the only way we’ll defeat them.”

Ezarius hesitates, looking from one god to another, then
throws a small pouch toward Meecra. Orfash yells and raises
an axe, Shorme shakes the ground with a slammed foot, and
Ohmlude picks up her fishing pole. Meecra gathers starlight
from the dusky sky and throws it at the other gods, who
charge at her.

Restore all health, stamina, and adventure cards. Gain adventure
card 100 (Meecra). Remove 1 status effect.

By now, Eugene has the engines roaring. You’re the last one
in the plane, and when you climb inside and shut the door,
you’re greeted by hopeful and fearful faces.

The plane shudders as it releases its grip on the yellow grass
and soars toward the giant face in the mountain and the God
Eye. The portal flickers as you grow closer, sputtering with the
last moments of its life.

A sudden screech shakes the plane and pierces your ears like
a knife. The Helthark’s titanic shadow climbs up the mountain
face; you can see it above you. It swoops down, eager to dash
your plane against the cliffs.

Then an eerie song drifts over the mountain. The Helthark
slows, flapping its wings laboriously. It drifts to the ground
with a massive thump, asleep, just as you fly through the
swirling mass of the God Eye. Home waits for you on the
other side. You can feel your heart racing. Have you finally
returned?

Clouds surround the plane. In a gap ahead, something tall and
majestic and red stands above a glittering sea; the Golden
Gate Bridge welcomes you back.

“We’re home!” Jessie calls, and a cheer erupts in the back of
the plane.

Miguel radios the airstrip, and by the time you land, Ruby,
Ed’s daughter, is waiting with teary eyes. She runs to Ed and
throws her arms around his neck, then dashes to Miguel, covering
him with kisses. You jump onto your motorcycle, waving
at them, and race away with a burst of speed, already searching
for the next adventure.
