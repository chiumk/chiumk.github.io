“The journal says the Starspear head is stuck down here in the
mouth of a terrifying beast,” says Miguel.

“After those spiders, what could be worse?” says Jessie,
heading down the cave.

You follow her into the dark, feeling your steps to keep from
slipping. A shaft up ahead lets in a wall of sunlight. The
opening breaks across a palace of spiring icicles, and beyond
it you glimpse the open water. Icebergs bask in the northern
sun-unmoving titans on a green sea.

As you gape at the view, something breathes deeply in the
cavern nearby-it’s a massive walrus. Your heart pounds in
your ears, but you soon realize the creature is sleeping. Something
shines near the base of its left tusk.

“Holy cats,” whispers Jessie.

“I think it’s a walrus, actually,” says Miguel. Jessie shoots him
an exasperated look.

“There’s the spearhead,” says Ezarius.
