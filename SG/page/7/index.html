14.5

14.5
Korman calls for Rascan, and a skinny man with a huge bush
of wild, singed hair appears at the doorway, blinking at you.

“Your paintings are genius,” says Miguel, barely masking his
excitement.

Rascan leans away, disgust curling his features. “You found
the cabin.”

“The paintings will be destroyed if they’re not moved. Why
don’t you sell them, at least, so they’ll be taken care of?”
Rascan frowns. “I hate those paintings. I felt something call
me to the mountains to paint them, but I left my wife behind.
Now her mother has poisoned her against me. I hope the
whole cabin gets buried in a landslide.”

“But they could have a profound impact,” says Miguel with a
grand wave of his hand.

A desperate, mad look fills Rascan’s glassy eyes. “I don’t want
them to have an impact. I was about to go and torch the place
myself.”

Miguel takes on a horrified expression. “Are you out of your
mind?” he says, gripping the artist’s sleeve.

“Who are you to tell me what to do with my own creations?
Leave me alone!” Rascan recoils and shoves him to the
ground.

Ed doesn’t flinch. “If the man doesn’t want them, we could
certainly find a place for them, and make a few bucks in the
process. Let’s find a buyer before he burns the place.”

“If I see any of you near the cabin, I’ll kill you,” snaps Rascan,
storming back to work.

Jessie extends a hand to Miguel, pulling him from the ground.
“I think Rascan should be able to decide what happens to the
art. It’s his art.”

“Once it’s been created, art doesn’t belong to the artist. It
belongs to everyone,” Miguel says, brushing coal dust from his
coat.

“As long as money changes hands, I think we agree,” says Ed,
nodding.

Complete quest 134. Gain quest 135. Return to the map.

15.2

15.2
You find the narrow path behind the squid statue and follow
it into an icy cavern. Before long, you emerge into a chamber
filled with neat stone houses and lit by flickering lanterns.
Scarlet spiders hang from drooping webs slung across the
ceiling, their fat abdomens shining like lacquered shells.

Miguel stares upward, unwilling to take another step. “That
spider could catch a sheep.”

Rat people scuffle from their houses, some holding spears and
crossbows, others holding half-woven silk sheets. They mutter
nervously at the sight of you. Legrak, the zarg rat you rescued
from a bison, swings down from a spiderweb. “These people
are friends,” he tells the other zarg rats. “They’ve promised to
help us.”

The rats watch you warily, but most of them lower their weapons.

“I’m surprised you made it here,” says Legrak. “I wouldn’t have
come if I were you.”

“The spiders-they’re not venomous, are they?” asks Miguel.

Legrak glances upward at a bulbous spider abdomen swaying
above. “What? These babies are harmless. They only eat cave
slugs. We harvest their webs to make silk. It’s beautiful stuff.”

For the first time, you notice the fine red silk Legrak wears.

“But we’re going to lose it all,” he says, sorrowfully. “The miners
at Tharkolm know we live here. The captain of the miners’
guild knows about the silk. He plans to come down here and
pillage all of it.”

“We can bargain with him. If you make a good offer, he’ll
listen,” says Ed.

“That may not be enough,” says Legrak. “But I hope it doesn’t
come to violence. We wouldn’t have much of a chance. We’re
just silk weavers, and our spiders are docile and defenseless.”

Legrak gives you some supplies. “The miners are at the top of
the canyon. Do what you can.”

Complete quest 60. Gain quest 67. Gain 1 bloodflower, 1 whiskey, 1
rope, and 1 meat. Return to the map.

15.3

15.3
The mining guild mercenaries camp in the heart of the canyon,
where ice grips the jagged walls, and every shadow cradles
a deep and healthy frost. Nearby, a giant statue cut into the
shape of a squid looms over the campsite-an unsettling
visage to scare away unwanted visitors. The guild captain
greets you. “Glad you joined us. Don’t mind the hideous
statue-we’re planning to destroy it sometime soon. We were
beginning to worry you wouldn’t show up.”

“The zarg rats are vicious?” asks Jessie.

“Oh, quite nasty. Very dangerous. That’s why we needed as
many mercenaries as possible.”

The guild captain whistles and the miners and mercenaries
advance, circling the statue and walking down a narrow path.

Before long, you emerge into a chamber filled with neat, stone
houses. Fat spiders hang from droopy webs on the ceiling.
Rat-people appear at the doors of the houses, some of them
wielding crossbows and spears, others gripping half-woven
sheets of silk. A panic breaks out at the sight of the mining
guild troops.

“Gather all the spider silk! I don’t want it damaged!” calls the
guild captain.

“Wait, we’re taking their property?” Miguel protests. “These
rats don’t look like a threat to me.”

“Quiet! Everyone fall in line!” shrieks the guild captain. The
miners charge the village.

15.6
The rats make a pitiful defense, rapidly giving out and escaping
into narrow caves near the back of the village. The miner
mercenaries brutalize the rats, chasing away every last one of
them.

The zarg rats gone, the mercenaries pillage the homes for woven
spider silk and anything else of value. A few of them cut
down the fat, docile spiders hanging above the village.

“The vermin will finally leave this canyon!” yells the guild
captain in his red coat, setting a roof on fire with a lit torch.
He pays you and sends you on your way.

“How low we’ve fallen,” says Miguel with disgust.
