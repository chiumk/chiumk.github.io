the low ceiling and step cautiously over the shallow pools of
water they feed. But the air is warm and humid-a welcome

tunnel and beckons you into the awesome sight-an underground
arena. To the north, glowing rivers of molten rock pour
from dark heights.

44.2

44.2
A falcon soars down from the clouds and lands atop the
belltower. It tilts its head with a penetrating stare, then dives
from the tower. It lands on a rock in the churchyard, wings
rippling outward, and transforms into a woman in a thick coat
of feathers. The skin around her eyes is stained black, starkly
outlining her white, pupilless eyes.

“I am Hessrah, daughter of the god Kemter,” she says. “I’ve
been watching you, and I know you seek totems for Meecra.
What devious plan she has for you, I do not know, but I warn
you not to trust her.”

“Trusting any of the gods is a dangerous game,” says Ezarius.

Hessrah returns the barest hint of a grin. “That sounds like
Red Heretic talk. Such disrespect would see you destroyed in
older times. But it doesn’t matter now. My sworn enemy, the
god Thrack, is venturing north. If you ever find yourself facing
him in combat, call my name, and I will do what I can to aid
you.”

“Why is he your enemy?” asks Jessie.

Hessrah sneers. “He stole my eggs for his depraved followers
to consume! I will tear out his eyes in payment.” In a burst of
feathers she turns back into a giant falcon, flying high into the
clouds until she disappears.

Complete quests 27 and 116. Gain quest 78. Draw 2 ability cards.
Return to the map.

44.5

44.5
The walls of the crypts are filled with grinning skulls and broken
skeletons. In a room at the end of the corridor, a skeleton
sits in a locked cage, staring at the floor with the weight of the
dead church and all the wild world outside on his shoulders.

“Hello. I’m Kresspin,” says the skeleton with a sigh.

“This is a trick,” says Miguel. “I saw a magician perform
something like this before.” Ed and Jessie keep their distance,
eyeing the moving bones with distrust.

“It’s no trick,” says the skeleton. “When I died, I clung to this
totem-its power remains, even after many years. It’s given me
life, of a sort.” He holds a totem out for you to see. “I’ve been
here for such a long time, unable to leave. I’ve thought about
throwing the totem and just embracing the afterlife, but I can’t
bring myself to do it.”

“We all must die, eventually,” says Ezarius.

“Yes, but I have something to live for,” says Kresspin. “I wish
to see my love again.”

A. Take the totem from Kresspin the skeleton.
(Turn to 44.6)

B. Unlock the cage and let Kresspin the skeleton out.

“I’m free after all these years! I can go on living!” says Kresspin.

“Yes, living,” says Ed, eyeing the skeleton up and down.

Kresspin picks up a tattered tunic and cape and puts them on.

“I must travel with you until my debt is repaid,” declares
Kresspin.

“Er, no thanks,” says Jessie. “I don’t know if you’d like my
cooking.”

“Not to worry!” says Kresspin. “I’m an excellent cook. I used
to cook for the queen of Stormlock City. She loved my cooking,
and I loved her. Too much, as it turned out. When the
king caught the two of us in an embrace, he had me executed
by a nest of flesh-eating beetles.”

“That’s an unfortunate way to die,” Miguel gulps.

“I should visit the queen again,” muses Kresspin.
