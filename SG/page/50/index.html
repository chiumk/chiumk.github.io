96.7
Bedi gives you a hug.

“My parents were both killed by King Trusiam. The king tried
to evict us from our farm because we couldn’t pay all his ridiculous
new taxes. My parents tried to resist, but he sent his
soldiers to take the farm by force and they were both killed. I
barely escaped.” Bedi wipes a tear from her eye. “That’s why
I feel so strongly that we need to get the word out to as many
people as possible. Everyone should know what that awful
man has done in the name of greed.”

Ezarius nods. “All those, be they god or mortal, who ruthlessly
rule over others with cruelty and greed must be stopped for
the good of all.”

Bedi smiles a little sadly. “I’m glad we see eye to eye. We can’t
pay you much, but I’ve been keeping this trinket on me for
sentimental reasons. Maybe you’ll find a better use for it.”

96.12
The creature whines at you pathetically as it takes its last few
struggling breaths.

Ed watches it pass with deep sadness in his eyes. “What an
odd yet noble creature.”

“It tried to kill us!” Jessie protests.

Ed nods. “Probably just a survival instinct. Who among us
hasn’t, at some point, lashed out at others in fear and desperation?
Or simply disregarded the wellbeing of others in
pursuit of our own selfish goals? I don’t think we were wrong
for killing it, it just seems a shame. I doubt there’s anything
else like it in any world you’d care to visit.”

You discover the creature’s nest behind the waterfall. It had
gathered a large assortment of random items over the long
years-including a variety of colored glass bottles, a broken
pair of spectacles, and a cherrywood desk chair. Hidden away
in a corner, beneath a pile of straw, you find several large eggs
that are still warm to the touch.

“I insist we take these eggs with us!” says Ed. “Such a
spectacular species deserves a fighting chance. Even if we
can’t care for them long-term ourselves, perhaps we can find
someone else who can.”

Complete quest 179. Gain quests 182 and 185. Gain adventure
card 126 (Stormlock Beast Eggs). Gain 2 gergon leaves and 1 ice
diamond.

97

97~
Just as you’re very nearly too frozen to go on, you find a
warm campfire. You rush to it eagerly before noticing who the
campfire belongs to.

“Welcome,” says a voice that is both rumbling and melodic.
The person sitting behind the fire is a tall, muscular woman
with the horns of a bull.

Ezarius curses, reaching for a weapon. “Are you an agent of
Orf ash?” he demands.

The woman sighs. “Orf ash is no friend of mine, although you
could say I’m an abandoned experiment of his. The only one
of my kind. Just like my friends here. Each the only one of its
kind.”

For the first time you notice the creatures. A chicken with a
snake for a tail sits at her feet. A creature with the front half
of a hawk and the back half of a house cat perches on her
shoulder. A white rabbit the size of a horse stands behind her,
blending seamlessly into the snow until it turns to look at you
with bright-orange eyes.

“The gods like to play at creation. They’re not always happy
with the results. But even we outcasts deserve to live, yes?
My name is Dura, and all are welcome at my fire. If you are not
busy, why not stay and share a meal with me?”

97.2

97.2
Ed brings out the eggs and Dura gasps, staring at them with
wonder and excitement.

“They are truly beautiful. You say the mother is gone?”

Ed’s face falls. “It was self-defense on our part, I’m afraid.”

Dura shrugs. “Such is the way of life. But you saved her children.
For that, I’m sure she would be grateful. As am I.”

“You’ll take them in, then?” asks Ed.

Dura nods enthusiastically. “Gladly! I will have to move on
to warmer climes, of course. At least until they are hatched. I
have plenty of experience piecing together an animal’s needs
from scratch, so I should be able to care for them well. But
please, I must give you something in return.”

Ed tries to refuse. “You’re doing me a favor by taking care of
them!”

“No, no, you did the world a great favor by bringing them this
far,” insists Dura. “I understand you are from another world,
but if the two of us ever cross paths again, I hope we will
greet each other as friends. And perhaps I will have a few new
beasts to introduce you to as well.”

Ed smiles sincerely. “I’ll look forward to it.”

Lose quest 185. Gain quest 186. Gain 1 torch and 1 ice diamond.
Cross off location 97. Return to the map.
