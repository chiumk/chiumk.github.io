27.2
In the deep woods, you find the camp where lmirian’s brother
told the conspirators to meet. Nervous harkians exchange
uncomfortable glances when you arrive.

“It’s well known that most don’t keep the speech rule. But
Ekeren would have us following his every command!”

“But he has too many with him. Many will die if we fight,” says
lmirian.

Horse cries shatter the circle of conspirators; Ekeren and
his followers surround you with sharp sickles and gleaming
spears.

“They must have followed us here!” says lmirian, snatching a
broadsword from the ground.

COMBAT 1, 2, 3, 4, 44 (Level 8)
If you are defeated, lose quest 32. Lose adventure card 37
(lmirian). Gain quest 35. Cross off location 27. Return to the map.
(If you defeat the enemies, continue reading.)

WIN : continue

With hell in his eyes, Ekeren leaps to deal a death blow to
lmirian, casting a bloody spear at him. lmirian spins around
and leaps out of the way, then Ekeren tumbles forward, falling
into a jagged trident. He struggles for breath, choking, reaching
upward. Then he’s still.

“Thank you for helping us. This was a gruesome way to end
things, but I believe we’ll have a free society now that we can
do away with the old laws,” says lmirian.

“Careful you do not abandon all your customs or you’ll find
yourselves lost in a stormy sea of ideas,” says Ezarius.

“Perhaps we will find the library one day,” says lmirian. “Here,
as promised for helping us.”

Complete quest 37. Gain quest 23. Lose adventure card 37
(lmirian). Gain 3 tundra berries and 1 jadeworm egg. Return to the
map.

28.3

28.3
You traverse the frozen path of the canyon floor as a cold
whisper trickles in your ear and down your spine. You turn,
but none of your companions are close enough to have done
it.

At the mouth of the canyon, two crooked columns of rock
mark the path to the permafrost tundra to the north. The
same cold whisper rides the biting wind, but this time it
sounds like a chorus of voices, though you can’t make out
what they’re saying.

As you turn back, you find your companions towered over by
shadowy ghosts, their piercing eyes like infernal gates to the
beyond.

“The boy,” you manage. “You took the boy, didn’t you?”

The ghosts whisper back.

28.4

28.4
This time, you understand their whispers. “We saved the boy.
He was freezing, so we led him on the path to the spirit world.
But now he won’t leave the path. He’s between worlds, and
he’s breaking it apart. It’s very painful for us; we won’t be able
to hold the path open forever.”

“Maybe we can talk to him,” says Jessie, more comfortable
with the spirits now. “How do we reach him?”

The spirits drift away to the right, toward the wall of the cliffs.
A white line pulses along the wall, like a lightning bolt stuck at
the edge of the rocks. The ghosts walk into it.

“Follow us. We will take you to him,” they say.

You walk toward the line. Your vision grows hazy. As you
touch the wall, everything swirls away from you-your whole
world liquefies and mixes together in a giant can of paint.

On the other side, you find him on the path to the spirit
world . It’s a barren path, but at least it’s not cold. The horizon
holds a tall mountain a great distance away. A young man sits
by a fire near the path, making coffee in a rusty kettle.

“You’re from Henrik’s plane, aren’t you, boy?” says Miguel.

The boy nods as if he expected this. “It’s Phillip, mister, and
I’m not going back there. I don’t know who you people are,
but I like it just fine right here.”

“We’re from San Francisco, and we’re trying to get back there,”
you say.

“The spirits that helped you say you’re causing them problems.
You can’t stay here,” says Jessie.

Phillip shakes his head and sips coffee from a tin mug. “Nope.
Don’t care. I feel much better in here, away from all the pain.
And I’m not going back to San Francisco, if that’s what you
have in mind.”

“Why won’t you go back? You want to sit here, in this?” Jessie
looks around at the barren landscape. Rainclouds hang low,
but you know they’ll never rain.

“It’s better than what I’ve got waiting for me at home. At least
I’m safe in here,” says Phillip. “Besides, time just doesn’t seem
to pass in here. I’m never bored. Never hungry. And the coffee
never runs out.”

“Come on, you can’t stay here forever,” says Jessie impatiently.

“Oh yes, I can. And if you leave me here, I’ll give you a dormant
totem I found,” says Phillip.

A. Use force to get the boy out.
~ STRENGTH 13
Fail: -11 health.
(Turn to 28.6)

B. Accept the totem payment.
(Turn to 28.7)
