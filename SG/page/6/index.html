12.2
You overpower the artist, bind his hands and feet, and gag
him with a wrinkled rag.

“What do we do with him?” asks Jessie. “Did anyone think
this through?”

“He was being irrational!” Miguel tears at his hair. “Artists
don’t destroy their work!”

“No,” says Ed. “They sell it. And when they don’t sell it, they
donate it for posterity, so let’s thank Rascan for his contribution
to future generations and leave.”

Miguel runs into the cabin and returns with a stack of canvases
and a small bag of supplies. “This was all I could carry,” he
moans.

You untie Rascan and leave him in the dirt, where he watches
you with hateful eyes. You retrace the mountain path and, as
you look back, you see a column of black smoke.

13~
“I sometimes wonder if you’re the right man for my daughter,”
says Ed. His smooth tone hides the tension growing in the
conversation.

Miguel clenches a fist, controlling his breath. “I’ve been nothing
but devoted to Ruby!”

“Devoted?” Ed says, accusingly. “Look at you! You love this.
New places, new people, new things to brag about to your
intellectual friends. Do you even care that she’s home worried
about you? Do you even want to get back?”

Miguel lets out an exasperated sigh. “Even if we do get back,
it’s not just me she worries about. What if the police finally
get enough evidence to convict you? How much worry is that
going to cause her?”

Ed grows silent-a dangerous quiet that says Miguel has gone
too far. He stands and walks away without another word.

A. Ask Ed to scout around so you can talk to Miguel.
(Turn to 13.1)

B. Ask Miguel to search for supplies while you talk to Ed.
(Turn to 13.2)

13.2

13.2
“Are you away from your daughter much?” you ask.

“When it’s your only child, any time away is too much.” Ed
sighs.

You search for a way to reassure him that he’ll make it home,
but you can’t stand making empty promises.

“She’s just like Miguel,” he sighs. “There’s a flame in both of
them: for each other, for their art, for life. I just can’t stand her
losing both of us.”

You place your hand on his, reassuringly, and he smiles.
“Thanks for listening, kid.” Then he smirks, “Between you and
me, if you’re faced with the choice between the two of us, I’m
going home to Ruby. Leave Miguel here to study the wildlife.”

You laugh together as Miguel returns with an armful of firewood.

Gain quest 43. Cross off location 13. Return to the map.
