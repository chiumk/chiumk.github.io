An ornate door is carved into the cliff face, covered in a script
that not even Ezarius can read. Instead of a doorknob, you
find the etched emblem of a one-eyed fish.

Miguel examines the emblem carefully, his eyes shining with
scholarly excitement. “I’ve been doing a bit of research on
local history, and I believe the one-eyed fish was a known
symbol of the old kingdom of Cadwahlyn. Could this point
towards the location of the lost castle of King Anfortas?”

Jessie squints at the poster she ripped from the streets of
Stormlock City. “The fine print here mentions something
about a chant that’s supposed to open the castle door. Should
we give it a try?”

When you press your ear to the stone you can hear the distant
sound of weeping.

94.2
You speak the words:

Fisher king,
Broken thing,
As he lies the kingdom dies.

Empty throne,
Tomb of stone,
Hear the cries and see him rise.

As you speak, the door begins to glow-softly at first and then
brighter and brighter until you’re forced to close your eyes.
When you open them again, the door is gone, leaving only an
empty cave entrance and stairs leading down.

Tentatively you set off down a long, dark tunnel. At first, you
and your crew laugh and joke and speculate about what you
might find below, but the weeping you heard faintly before
grows louder as you descend, and your crew grows quieter,
until all you can hear are the gentle sobs and the thud of your
boots on the stone.

At the bottom of the steps, another massive stone door blocks
the tunnel.

Miguel holds up a torch to get a better look at the door. “This
one isn’t sealed,” he says, pushing lightly against the stone.
“It’s just heavy.”

You put out your torches. You don’t need them here. The
distant walls glow with a soft light that bathes everything in
a warm overtone, like the pages of an old book. The sound

you can see no rivers or waterfalls. The gates of the
castle lie wide open before you.

94.5

94.5
As you walk past the long-empty cells of the castle dungeon,
the shade of old King Anfortas walks with you. He holds a
large goblet of jeweled gold in his hands. He speaks in a clear,
authoritative voice.

“Come to me, Manawydan. The gods entrusted me with this
blessing, with this curse, which I must now bestow upon
you. Take it from me, my old foe and my old friend. Hide it in
your belly. I would not ask this awful thing of you if necessity
did not dictate it. I beg of you to save my ignorant kingdom
from the awful temptation of immortality and all the pain that
would come with it. A being that cannot die is a being that
cannot grow. I am asking you to take this burden on yourself,
to save humanity from becoming rotten and festering as a
stagnant pond.”

The shade disappears as you step up to the edge of a deep
underground river rushing loudly through the center of the
dungeon. It’s a jarring contrast to the castle. You’re considering
how you might cross the tumbling waters when a creature-
rotten and festering as a stagnant pond-leaps out at
you from the river.

Turn to BOSS 11 on the opposite page.

The defeated creature shrinks, reversing its transformation
until all that remains on the ground below you is a snow-white
stag. A ghostly version of the stag stands, leaving its body
behind. Across the clearing you see the ghost of young King
Anfortas, bow-and-arrow in hand, ready to loose an arrow

for a long moment before King Anfortas lowers his bow and
the ghost of the stag bounds away into the woods.

“A true King must embody mercy as well as strength,” Anfortas
says to himself, as if reciting a lesson.

95.3
Jessie blows on the horn and a deafening blare sounds out
across the forest, scaring the birds from a nearby tree.

For a long moment, everything is still.

Suddenly, from behind, a snow-white stag leaps past you.
Time seems to slow as the stag hangs in the air, showing off
its ethereal beauty. Then you blink, and the creature quickly
disappears into the forest.

Before you can react, more figures burst from the woodslaughing
noblemen, barking dogs, and braying horses-all of
them raucous and joyful in their pursuit of the elusive stag.
They pass through the clearing like a whirlwind and disappear
again just as quickly.

“I do say, it looks as though the hunt will be splendid again
this year.” Ed’s voice doesn’t quite sound like his own.

“Indeed,” Miguel responds. “Now if only that young buck Anfortas
could actually catch us a young buck to display on the
castle wall, then we’d really have something to celebrate.”

Ed and Miguel laugh heartily for a moment and then stop
abruptly.

Miguel covers his mouth with his hands, a frightened expression
in his eyes. “Why did I just say that?”

Jessie grins. “Maybe you’re being possessed? We are dealing
with ghosts after all.”
