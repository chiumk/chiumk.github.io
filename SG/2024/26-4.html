26.4
There are narrow footprints in the mud on the top of the arch.

“Perhaps these belong to the owner of the suitcase,” says Ed.

At the ridge on the far side of the arch, you look around, but
see no one.

“Anyone missing some luggage? Got your claim ticket?” says
Miguel with a chuckle.

A muffled voice calls out. “I’m here!”

You follow the sound to a soggy pit. A spectacled woman in a
torn dress sits forlornly at the bottom. Ezarius reaches down a
large hand and pulls her up.

“Oh, thank goodness. Who are you people? How did you
know I was here?”

“We found your suitcase,” says Miguel, offering the muddy
case.

“My husband is here too. He wandered over here after we had
an argument. I was looking for him when I fell into the pit.”

“I’m sure he’s somewhere around here. We’ll find him,” says
Ezarius.
