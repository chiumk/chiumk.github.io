40.6
A bored man stands on a street corner, selling fur hats and old
shoes with wilted effort. You ask him about lost passengers
from Henrik’s plane while Ed tosses dice in the street, gambling
with a few city guards. Ed gets further behind, betting
what he doesn’t have. Soon, the guards demand payment and
Ed backs away, his hands spread.

One guard throws down the dice, sending them tumbling into
a crack in the street. “Pay up or we’ll arrest you!”

“One more game? Let’s keep playing,” says Ed.

“No! Pay us now!”

“Ed, why did you have to play with them?” says Miguel urgently,
looking for an escape route.

“We could fight ‘em for it,” whispers Jessie.

“No, there are too many here in the city,” hisses Miguel. “We
can’t start a fight. Too many people are watching.”

box.
