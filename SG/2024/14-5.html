Korman calls for Rascan, and a skinny man with a huge bush
of wild, singed hair appears at the doorway, blinking at you.

“Your paintings are genius,” says Miguel, barely masking his
excitement.

Rascan leans away, disgust curling his features. “You found
the cabin.”

“The paintings will be destroyed if they’re not moved. Why
don’t you sell them, at least, so they’ll be taken care of?”
Rascan frowns. “I hate those paintings. I felt something call
me to the mountains to paint them, but I left my wife behind.
Now her mother has poisoned her against me. I hope the
whole cabin gets buried in a landslide.”

“But they could have a profound impact,” says Miguel with a
grand wave of his hand.

A desperate, mad look fills Rascan’s glassy eyes. “I don’t want
them to have an impact. I was about to go and torch the place
myself.”

Miguel takes on a horrified expression. “Are you out of your
mind?” he says, gripping the artist’s sleeve.

“Who are you to tell me what to do with my own creations?
Leave me alone!” Rascan recoils and shoves him to the
ground.

Ed doesn’t flinch. “If the man doesn’t want them, we could
certainly find a place for them, and make a few bucks in the
process. Let’s find a buyer before he burns the place.”

“If I see any of you near the cabin, I’ll kill you,” snaps Rascan,
storming back to work.

Jessie extends a hand to Miguel, pulling him from the ground.
“I think Rascan should be able to decide what happens to the
art. It’s his art.”

“Once it’s been created, art doesn’t belong to the artist. It
belongs to everyone,” Miguel says, brushing coal dust from his
coat.

“As long as money changes hands, I think we agree,” says Ed,
nodding.
