7 [Q}
If keyword SALAMANDER, turn to 7.6.

Narrow boulders wear moss like satin nightgowns, at their
feet a set of gaping caves. You and Jessie decide to take a
look. You catch her as she misses her step on a patch of loose
gravel.

“Thanks, Claire. I’m sure Grandpa would be glad you’re here
watching out for me.”

“That’s a laugh!” you say. “George thought I was irresponsible.”

“No, he always liked you better than me,” Jessie replies. “I
asked him so many times to teach me how to fly. You know
what he said?”

“That it’s too dangerous?”

“That I didn’t have the brains for it,” Jessie kicks a nearby
stone, sending it hurtling down the slope. “And then he taught
you.”

A. Tell her you’ll teach her to fly.
(Turn to 7.1)

B. Tell her she should have stood up for herself.
(Turn to 7.2)

C. Say something mean about her grandfather.
(Turn to 7.3)
