deposits made smooth by eons of tiny drips. There’s a halo of
menacing orange hanging around one of the passages, and
you sneak up to find a room with slick walls, lanterns lit across
its low ceiling. It’s filled with bandits encircling a table and
muttering over a wrinkled map. Curved swords hang at their
belts and grotesque scars cover their faces. Stacks of crates
rest on lush, stained rugs, and in an adjacent room, you catch
sight of plush beds and ornate mirrors.
