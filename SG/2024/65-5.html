Thadri holds her hands up in a sign of surrender.

“Alright, alright. I don’t want any trouble. I got the pretty picture
off a man named Tekla. Real squirmy fellow, but I’ve had
dealings with him before and he always delivers what he says
he will. He was bragging about all the art he’d recently found.
Said he was selling off some of the pieces at a gallery show
near Milius. Art collectors from the south travel hundreds of
miles just to buy art there.”

Ed watches Thadri’s face carefully. “This Tekla, did you see if
he had a piece called Woman with Hairpin?”

Miguel gasps. “You were planning to sell Woman with Hairpin?
Why didn’t you tell me? You know how important that
painting is to Ruby.”

“I regret it now,” snaps Ed. “That’s why I need to get it back.”

Thadri glares as them both. “I don’t know any specifics. If you
want to know more, you’ll have to ask Tekla. Here, if I give you
this will you please go away and stop disrupting my business?”
