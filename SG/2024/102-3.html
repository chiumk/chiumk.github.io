102.3
The pilgrims give the mud seer a silver necklace with a cut
sapphire.

“Refined metal offends me,” says the mud woman. “I cannot
share wisdom with so little inspiration.”

“But we journeyed so far!” says one of the pilgrims.

“Fine. Here is a little wisdom. Let your daughter make her own
choices. With the gods weakened, you now have a chance
for real freedom. Do not bind the hands of those you love, for
love will turn cold.”

The pilgrims bow to the mud woman but look unsatisfied.
They leave, mumbling to themselves.

The mud woman turns to you. “For you, I offer a vision without
an offering. I see you are from a distant world. You seek
the totems of the gods, and there is one lost in a red desert to
the south. You’ll find it beneath the blind eye.”

A sudden vision fills your mind. A bird flies from this cave,
soaring over mountains to the south, until it lands on a dead
tree in rocky desert filled with red cliffs.

The woman descends into the muddy pool until all that remains
are ripples.
