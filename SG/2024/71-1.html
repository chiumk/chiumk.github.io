71.1
A swarm of sand bees whirls around a white hive. You make
your way north toward the river and Miguel rests a reassuring
hand on Flora’s shoulder.

“Don’t worry, we’ll find your husband soon.”

“What happened to him?” asks Jessie. “Why did he leave you
there in that pit?”

Flora adjusts her beach hat with a troubled expression, pausing
to collect herself; tears wait at the corners of her eyes.
“He didn’t leave me there. Horace and I, well, we haven’t been
getting along. We were having one of our arguments again,
and he said he needed a walk to cool down.”

“Bad idea, in this place,” says Ed. “Everything here wants to
kill us.”

“Yes, I know. But he’s a stubborn man. Once he’s made his
mind up, it’s so difficult to get him to change it.”

At the edge of the trees, a leafless branch extends from the
ferns like a clawed hand. The tattered remains of a suit coat
hang from its fingers. “That’s Horace’s coat!”

You follow Horace’s footprints and after an hour of searching,
a voice cries, “Flora! Watch out! Keep back!”

A middle-aged man with a bald head stands near the edge of
a cliff. In his hand he holds an egg the size of a football. Two
feathered dinosaurs circle him, snarling and snapping their
raptor jaws, closing in patiently but with a primal certainty.

Flora dashes forward but slams to a stop when another raptor
appears on a ledge above. It watches her with a mixture of
curiosity and hunger.

“Put that egg down, now!” she scolds.

“I’m starving, and we need food,” Horace replies. “And there
are even more eggs in their nest!”

“You’re going to get yourself killed!”

“No, no. They’re just like ostriches,” Horace sounds confident.
“They can’t hurt us. Just scare them away.”

The raptors circle closer. One of them turns on Flora, and she
backs toward the edge of the cliff. She’s about to fall over a
sheer drop.
