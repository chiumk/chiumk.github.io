“A woman by the lake sure got our attention,” you say. “I
could see half her organs through her bare ribcage! She said
Meecra was keeping things from us.”

Henrik laughs. “You’ll soon learn not to trust this place.
Everything and everyone here is mad. It’s a tangle of myth
and magic which I do not pretend to understand. But I can tell you
one thing. I’ve had enough of it. I want to go home and see my children.”

“It must be torture sitting here wondering if you’ll see them
again,” says Ed, and he and Henrik fall into somber silence.
