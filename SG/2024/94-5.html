94.5
As you walk past the long-empty cells of the castle dungeon,
the shade of old King Anfortas walks with you. He holds a
large goblet of jeweled gold in his hands. He speaks in a clear,
authoritative voice.

“Come to me, Manawydan. The gods entrusted me with this
blessing, with this curse, which I must now bestow upon
you. Take it from me, my old foe and my old friend. Hide it in
your belly. I would not ask this awful thing of you if necessity
did not dictate it. I beg of you to save my ignorant kingdom
from the awful temptation of immortality and all the pain that
would come with it. A being that cannot die is a being that
cannot grow. I am asking you to take this burden on yourself,
to save humanity from becoming rotten and festering as a
stagnant pond.”

The shade disappears as you step up to the edge of a deep
underground river rushing loudly through the center of the
dungeon. It’s a jarring contrast to the castle. You’re considering
how you might cross the tumbling waters when a creature-
rotten and festering as a stagnant pond-leaps out at
you from the river.
