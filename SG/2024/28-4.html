28.4
This time, you understand their whispers. “We saved the boy.
He was freezing, so we led him on the path to the spirit world.
But now he won’t leave the path. He’s between worlds, and
he’s breaking it apart. It’s very painful for us; we won’t be able
to hold the path open forever.”

“Maybe we can talk to him,” says Jessie, more comfortable
with the spirits now. “How do we reach him?”

The spirits drift away to the right, toward the wall of the cliffs.
A white line pulses along the wall, like a lightning bolt stuck at
the edge of the rocks. The ghosts walk into it.

“Follow us. We will take you to him,” they say.

You walk toward the line. Your vision grows hazy. As you
touch the wall, everything swirls away from you-your whole
world liquefies and mixes together in a giant can of paint.

On the other side, you find him on the path to the spirit
world . It’s a barren path, but at least it’s not cold. The horizon
holds a tall mountain a great distance away. A young man sits
by a fire near the path, making coffee in a rusty kettle.

“You’re from Henrik’s plane, aren’t you, boy?” says Miguel.

The boy nods as if he expected this. “It’s Phillip, mister, and
I’m not going back there. I don’t know who you people are,
but I like it just fine right here.”

“We’re from San Francisco, and we’re trying to get back there,”
you say.

“The spirits that helped you say you’re causing them problems.
You can’t stay here,” says Jessie.

Phillip shakes his head and sips coffee from a tin mug. “Nope.
Don’t care. I feel much better in here, away from all the pain.
And I’m not going back to San Francisco, if that’s what you
have in mind.”

“Why won’t you go back? You want to sit here, in this?” Jessie
looks around at the barren landscape. Rainclouds hang low,
but you know they’ll never rain.

“It’s better than what I’ve got waiting for me at home. At least
I’m safe in here,” says Phillip. “Besides, time just doesn’t seem
to pass in here. I’m never bored. Never hungry. And the coffee
never runs out.”

“Come on, you can’t stay here forever,” says Jessie impatiently.

“Oh yes, I can. And if you leave me here, I’ll give you a dormant
totem I found,” says Phillip.

A. Use force to get the boy out.

Fail: -11 health.
