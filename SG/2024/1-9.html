“We’ve been robbed!” Ed stands next to an open crate entirely devoid of any valuable artworks. “All the crates are empty!
I’m ruined!”

“No!” shouts Miguel, searching around the empty crates as
though the missing art could have merely been misplaced.
“How could this happen?”

Ezarius approaches, looking mildly concerned. “What has
been stolen?”

“Art,” explains Jessie, also searching around for potential
clues. “You know, paintings and stuff. Ed makes a living as an
art trader. He had a big sale lined up in Honolulu.”

“I see,” says Ezarius, his tone dismissive. “This is about money, then. Aren’t there more important things to worry about
right now? Like getting you all back home?’’

“It’s not about money!” snaps Ed. “There was a-“ Ed glances
at Miguel, looking suddenly guilty. “Never mind, we just… we
need to get my stolen property back as soon as possible. This
has to be our top priority. I insist on it.”

You agree to keep an eye out the next time you’re at a market,
to see if you can catch anyone attempting to sell any of the
stolen pieces.
