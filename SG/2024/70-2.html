70.2
In the heart of the shrine, mist gathers and swirls, and voices
mutter beyond the mist, echoing from a great distance.
You think of the zombie woman in your mind and reach out
a hand. Her hand appears from the mist, and you pull her
through. Her transparent form is hazy in the candlelight.

“Ah! Back to the world of the living.”

“This is dark magic,” says Miguel, backing away from the
ghost woman.

“Nonsense! I was not ready to depart. I have revenge to take.”

“Revenge? I thought you were going to tell us a secret about
Meecra,” says Jessie.

“Oh? Yes, there is something about her that puts you all in
danger,” the woman cackles. “When the other gods find out,
they’ll try to destroy Meecra, and you,” she cackles again.

Jessie frowns. “How would you know something like that?”

The woman wears a wicked grin. “I found Meecra in the spirit
world, and she was talking to herself.”

Ed breaths an exasperated sigh. “Well? What did she say?”

The woman folds her arms, her hazy figure flickering in the
candlelight. “Meecra is the one responsible for the downfall of
the gods. And they’re going to kill her for it.”

Ezarius shakes his head. “No, you’re dead wrong there.”

“Well, I’m dead, thanks to Henrik, but I’m not wrong,” says the
woman.

“Henrik killed you?”

“Yes, he did, and I thank you for bringing me back here so
that I can haunt him.”

With a cackle, the ghost woman dashes through a wall.

“No! Come back!” says Jessie, but the woman is gone.

“I generally discourage summoning spirits,” says the monk.
“But it seemed like you people were desperate. I guess ·you
didn’t know what you were getting into.”

Jessie wears a troubled expression. “Do you think she was
telling the truth? About Henrik?”

“Let’s hope not,” says Ed. “Either way, we need to warn him, if
we can.”
