31.1
A woman passes by carrying a basket of berries, her face
round and blushed. Tears run down her cheeks, splashing
onto the tiny, blue fruits.

“What’s the matter, if you don’t mind my asking?” says Ed.

The woman sets down the basket, drying her eyes with a
handkerchief.

“It’s none of your business. Who are you, anyway?”

“We’re new in town. We’re looking for some boys from Locif a
Ironworks. Seen them around here?”

The woman’s features soften. “Locifa? Did Korman send you?”

“Yes, have you seen his sons?” asks Miguel.

She hesitates, then says, “I was going to be married to Tern,
the oldest. But he wanted me to move away from here, from
my family. I don’t want to move. I’ve lived here my whole life.”

Jessie shakes her head. “He just expects you to fall in line
once you’re married?”

“No, he’s not like that. It’s complicated. We got in an argument
and he stormed off, to the west of town. But his brother can’t
find him, and I’m worried.”
