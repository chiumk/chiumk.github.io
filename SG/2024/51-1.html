51.1 ~
King Trusiam sits on a tall throne at the end of a long hallway,
the architecture so exaggeratedly large that it’s more ridiculous
than grand. As you draw closer, you can see that his
crown is too large for him and hangs low over one side of his
forehead. He sits crookedly on his throne, body bent with age,
the heavy layers of fine fabrics he wears weighing him down
further. But his eyes are clear and sharp, and his expression is
haughty and full of pride.

You bow before the king, but he does not respond. Instead,
another man, not quite as bent with age, stands and greets
you. He introduces himself as Count Heilynn, descendant of
a long line of nobility stretching back to the old Cadwahlyn
kingdom itself.

“Have you come to fulfill the king’s quests?” The count looks
just as haughty and proud as the king.
