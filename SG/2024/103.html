You land the plane on a barren plateau, fighting against wicked
winds and an uneven runway. A sour exhilaration fills your
stomach the few seconds before the wheels hit the ground,
but the craft soon comes to a stop near the rim of the canyon.

“A terrifying contraption,” says lmirian, stumbling out of the
plane. He had spent the entire flight huddled in a corner. “I
doubt I’ll ever grow comfortable with it.”

“How can you say that about our baby?” says Eugene, patting
the riveted steel like it’s a fat puppy.

Dust whips over the edge of the vast canyon, catching in rows
of shale. Columned ruins line a ledge far below. You can’t tell
how deep the canyon goes; its bottom is shrouded in cold
mist.
