9~
If keyword LIBRARY, turn to 9.1.

A horse-headed traveler approaches you, bowing slowly with
his arms spread to show he’s no threat.

“Greatness awaits you, strangers. My name is lmirian, harkian
scholar and philosopher.”

“You’ve practically used up all your words for the day already.
Do you not follow the speaking laws of the harkians?” asks
Ezarius.

“Do you know much of our ways, Lukran?” lmirian whinnies.

“They say a harkian utters words like a miser spends silver,”
says Ezarius.

The horse-headed man sighs. “Not many harkians can obey
the word law-twenty words each day is unbearable. It’s
outdated and inconvenient. The people say it’s a command
from our creator, Orf ash, but that’s a lie. Why then do not our
cousins, the mythans, also have a daily speech limit?”

Ezarius shrugs. “I can’t imagine Orfash caring about such
things. Besides, the gods have lost their power. You need not
fear them.”

“I do not, but my village does. There’s a harkian named Ekeren
who has declared himself a speaker for the gods. He says
he’s been called to return everyone to the true path, which is,
according to him, obeying the speech laws.”

“Sounds like he doesn’t obey the speech limit himself,” says
Miguel.

lmirian grunts. “He says that the law doesn’t apply to him.
He’s wanted to rule these lands for years. But everyone would
stop following him if they knew the truth. There’s a library of
ancient harkian tomes that tell the truth of our history. Help
me find them. I’ve heard of you people, and I know you’re
looking for totems. I have one, and you can have it if you help
me find the library. But first, we need an ancient tablet from
my village. It’s hidden somewhere in Ekeren’s house, but he
won’t let anyone see it. We should start there.”

Gain quest 32. Gain adventure card 37 (lmirian). Return to the
map.
